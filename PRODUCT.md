# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Arabic-speaking Muslims managing personal finances through an Android app. Primary users are financially-conscious individuals who want Islamic-compliant money management — budgeting, expense tracking, zakat calculation, debt management, and long-term planning (FIRE). They use the app daily for transaction logging and periodic reviews of budgets, investments, and goals.

## Product Purpose

Fajrak is an Islamic personal finance management app that helps users track income/expenses, manage budgets and debts, calculate zakat, plan for financial independence (FIRE), and learn Islamic financial principles — all with real-time sync across mobile and web via Supabase. Success means users gain clarity and confidence in their financial lives while staying aligned with Islamic values.

## Brand Personality

Trustworthy. Clear. Islamic.

The app communicates financial confidence through precise numbers and generous whitespace (Stripe aesthetic). It feels authoritative but approachable — never playful, never cold. The Islamic identity is felt through design choices (gold accent, lesson card aesthetic) rather than overt labeling.

## Anti-references

The DESIGN.md defines the visual direction through three curated sources (Stripe, Apple HIG, Material 3) and their explicit don't-take lists. Avoid: generic SaaS dashboards, fintech navy-and-gold clichés, glassmorphism as default, gradient text, card shadows, and any visual pattern that undermines financial data confidence.

## Design Principles

1. **Numbers are heroes.** Every screen centers on financial data that feels large, confident, and authoritative. Never show a number unless it is accurate — blank is better than stale.

2. **Borders, not shadows.** Structure through precision (1px borders, zero elevation) rather than decorative weight. Trust is earned through clarity, not depth.

3. **Progressive disclosure.** Show only what the user needs right now. The first view of any screen should fit without scrolling for the most common use case. Depth is one tap away, never forced.

4. **Interruptions must be earned.** Non-blocking patterns (toasts, inline messages, bottom sheets) are the default. Full modals only for irreversible decisions.

5. **Islamic identity — felt, not labeled.** The app's values come through in color, typography, and interaction patterns, not through repeated Islamic phrases in the UI.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA 2.1 minimum. Contrast ratios of 4.5:1 for body text, 3:1 for large text. Full TalkBack support for all interactive elements. Support system text scaling up to 200%. Support reduced motion preferences. Test every screen in both light and dark mode before shipping.
